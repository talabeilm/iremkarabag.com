About

I am an Associate Professor of English at York University, where I research the intersections of language, identity, and power in postcolonial and contemporary literature. My work bridges literary scholarship and social justice, examining how narrative shapes — and is shaped by — the struggles of marginalized communities.

Beyond the classroom, I am an active advocate for immigrant language rights, community literacy, and equity in education. I believe that literature and language are not only subjects of study but vital tools for social transformation.

Research Interests: Language and Identity in Postcolonial Literature, Narrative and Social Justice, Multilingualism in Literary Texts, Critical Pedagogy
